Scrambled Eggs

Description

Scrambled eggs is a dish made (usually) with chicken eggs that are stirred, whipped, or beaten together with either salt, butter, and oil (or some combination of those ingredients). It is then heated until formed into curds.

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup half-n-half
  • 1/4 teaspoon Kosher salt
  • black pepper (for serving)
  • fresh chopped herbs, for serving

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, half-n-half and salt until the mixture is uniform making sure there is no streaks of yolk or whites.
  2. Melt butter in a small nonstick pan over medium heat, until the butter coats the whole pan just starts to foam.
  3. Add the eggs to the center of the pain and reduce the heat to medium-low
  4. As the edges begin to form, using a rubber spatula, push the eggs from one end to the other. Repeat this process, pausing in between swipes to allow the uncooked eggs to settle on the pan and cook.
  5. When the eggs are mostly cooked with big pillow-y folds, slowly fold the eggs into itself.
  6. Remove from the heat when eggs still shimmer with moisture.
  7. Transfer to serving plate and fiinish with cracked pepper.
